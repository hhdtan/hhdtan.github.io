The Transshipment Project: Devise a way to optimise the fuel economy of the NL transshipment project.


Let's say we have a very important meeting to attend, and we cannot afford to be late. If the goal was purely to not be late, then a naive solution would be to arrive as early as possible, and then wait at the venue. During our transit to the venue, we would travel at maximum speed (well within vehicular and regulatory limits, obviously) and that would be a solution to the problem. It is not a fantastic solution, but it is a solution.

What are some obvious problems with this methodology?

  1. Arriving too early serves no practical purpose. Early is good, but only if its early within reason
  2. Maximum speed is usually not an efficient mode of travel, it burns more fuel than it necessary (unless we're going to be late, there's no justifiable reason to book it)

Unfortunately, it is the solution employed by the transshipment side of things here to to avoid curtailment. The transshipment project is all about applying intelligence to the decision making process of this scheduling system.
