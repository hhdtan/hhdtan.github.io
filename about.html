Little Derrick & Magic

When Little Derrick was first introduced to computer science (CS) in 2017, he thought to himself: (oh god that's almost a decade ago)
"wait what? I can do this for a living?!"

That class, ICS4U, was my first introduction to the world of CS, and it is probably my favourite class to date, mainly because I had the world's goofiest lecturer, and, you get to program your very own flash game (ActionScript 3.0) – I made a little platformer game featuring a penguin and it's inverted counterpart, guin. "How would I build this software?

More importantly, previously-mundane tasks had suddenly become a puzzle. Programming has been one of my favourite things since, it felt like solving a maliciously-compliant puzzle that was constantly sassing me. (see below: Derrick Discovers Casting)

Miscellaneous Thoughts & Stories

Lackadaisical

Do you have a favourite word? Mine is... lackadaisical.

I was once described as such by someone dear to me, almost a lifetime ago, and today I revel at how increasingly relevant it's become. If you're unfamiliar with the term, it means 'carelessly lazy'. While it may not be the most appealing of portrayals, it was very... 'me'. I believe that my entire fascination for all things programming, AI/ML, and automation, stems from this nature of mine.

See, as a victim of long, drawn-out, repetitive tasks (also known as your first internship), I absolutely dread the idea of doing these monotonous tasks. I'd much rather spend hours trying create an automated solution, even if doing it manually would only take me 30 minutes. It matters not how much time I have, I will never willingly do things manually. (unless a quick cost-benefit analysis dictated necessity)

Engagement. The main difference was engagement. This was one of those things I've come to emphasize a lot, after spending much time pondering about "what makes a great lesson?"

Lackadaisical was an especially apt description of who I was at the time. I was hounded with so much workload that laying back for a breather felt like a luxury I couldn't afford. I was constantly drowning in work, so much so that favourite pastime became... doing nothing. It was even better if it was doing nothing with company.
